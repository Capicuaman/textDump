## ELECTROLYTES

**How can I tell if I am dehydrated and need electrolytes?**
Ideally, you want to hydrate with Ultima before you feel dehydrated and low on electrolytes. Signs of low electrolytes and dehydration can be easy to miss or blame on something else such as hunger, jet lag or a hangover. Feeling irritable, sluggish, tired, and having a headache or brain fog can actually be due to dehydration and an electrolyte imbalance. Leg cramps, diarrhea or constipation can also be signs of dehydration. Rehydrating with electrolytes can help these symptoms go away.

**What are electrolytes? Why are they so important?**
Electrolytes are minerals your body needs to function properly, such as sodium, calcium, chloride, magnesium, potassium and phosphorus. These minerals are able to conduct your body's electrical impulses and carry energy. This makes them crucial for regulating nerve and muscle function, converting glucose (sugar) to energy, and delivering oxygen to muscles and other cells for regular cell function and cell repair. Electrolytes also help maintain the balance of fluids in your cells, acidity (pH level) in your blood, and your blood pressure. Many fruits and vegetables contain electrolytes, and Ultima drink mixes contain a balance of electrolytes, without too much sodium, which is perfect for everyday hydration.

**When do we use electrolytes?**
Your body is always using electrolytes, even when you sleep. When you are active, exercising or outdoors in the heat, your body uses and loses electrolytes more quickly. In dry environments like an office building or on a plane, your electrolytes work harder to keep you hydrated. Your brain uses electrolytes to think and conduct the nerve impulses that drive the rest of your body to move and function. Because electrolytes play a role in so many of our bodily functions, signs of dehydration and low electrolytes can include headaches, leg cramps, irritability, fatigue and sluggishness. Because carbohydrates are often a good source of electrolytes, if you are on a low-carb diet like the ketogenic diet, you'll want to be more intentional about replenishing your electrolytes from drinks like Ultima and other food sources. If you're someone who stays active and regularly exercises, you'll want to stay hydrated before, during and after exercise so that your body has access to the important electrolytes needed for proper muscle function.

**What do each of the six electrolytes do for my body?**
-   **Calcium:** Your body needs calcium to move your muscles and to keep a regular heartbeat. It helps your nerves carry messages between your brain and body, and helps with blood flow allowing blood vessels to tighten and relax as needed.
-   **Chloride:** Chloride helps balance body fluids by regulating the amount of fluids, oxygen and nutrients going in and out of cells.
-   **Magnesium:** This vital mineral plays a key role in balancing blood sugar levels, converting glucose to energy, metabolizing fat, regulating muscle and nerve function, and it helps the body properly use other vitamins and minerals.
-   **Phosphorus:** The second most abundant mineral in the body after calcium, phosphorus is needed for your body's energy, plus the growth, maintenance and repair of all your body's cells and tissues. It helps balance blood pH levels, activates enzymes and regulates the function of nerves and muscles, including the heart.
-   **Potassium:** Potassium and sodium work together to maintain fluid balance in the body, and like sodium, you lose potassium through urine and sweat. It plays a role in how nerves transmit and muscles contract, including keeping the heartbeat regular. It also helps move nutrients and waste around your body's cells.
-   **Sodium:** Sodium helps your body retain water, balance ions and remove excess carbon dioxide. It works with potassium to balance fluids and expand and contract muscles. Sodium also helps regulate blood pressure and blood volume. Too much sodium can result in high blood pressure, and too little sodium can cause muscle cramps and dehydration.

**Why does Ultima Replenisher have lower sodium and higher potassium and magnesium?**
Most Americans get enough salt in their diet, and because we wanted our drink mixes to be good for daily use, we limited the sodium. Potassium and sodium work together to balance the fluids in our cells. Too much sodium can actually counteract potassium, preventing water (and nutrients) from absorbing into our cells. More than half of Americans do not get enough magnesium in their diet, and magnesium is key for so many nerve functions, bone health and blood sugar regulation. With these facts in mind, we formulated Ultima to have a meaningful and healthy balance of electrolytes in our drink mixes.
