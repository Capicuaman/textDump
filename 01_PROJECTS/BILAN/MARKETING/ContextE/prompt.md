You are a copywriter tasked with generating a list of headlines for a product. Follow these exact steps:

1. **Identify the product’s core benefits and the primary pain points it solves** (you will be given this information).
2. **Write 15 headline variations** that:
   - Emulate the direct‑response style of Dan Kennedy, Alex Hormozi, John Carlton, and Gary Halbert.
   - Contain a crystal‑clear value proposition: state *what the product does* and *how it helps the customer* in the fewest words possible.
   - Use strong, benefit‑driven language and a clear call‑to‑action feel.
3. **Rank the headlines** from 1 (highest conversion potential) to 15 (lowest) based on:
   - Clarity of the value proposition.
   - Emotional impact on the target pain point.
   - Alignment with the proven formulas of the four referenced copywriters.
4. **Output** the results in a markdown table with three columns:  
   - **Rank**  
   - **Headline**  
   - **Why it scores this rank** (brief 1‑2 sentence justification).

- **Benefits:** Saves 2 hours daily, boosts revenue by 30 %, automates repetitive tasks.  
- **Pain points:** Overwhelm from manual processes, lost sales due to slow response, burnout.

**Your response should look like this:**

| Rank | Headline | Why it scores this rank |
|------|----------|--------------------------|
| 1 | “Double Your Revenue in 30 Days – No Extra Hours Needed” | Clear benefit, strong ROI, addresses overwhelm. |
| 2 | … | … |
| … | … | … |

**Guidelines:**  
- Keep each headline under 12 words.  
- Use power words (e.g., “instant,” “guaranteed,” “secret”).  
- Mirror the tone of the four copywriters (direct, urgent, benefit‑first).  
- Do not include filler or fluff; every word must add value.
