### PLATFORM SPECIFICATIONS

#### 1. LINKEDIN (Professional Authority)
**Format:**
- Length: 1,200-1,500 characters (NOT words)
- First line: Compelling hook (under 120 characters - visible before "see more")
- Body: 3-4 short paragraphs
- End with: Clear CTA and 3-5 relevant hashtags
- Tone: Professional but conversational
- Include: Data/statistics to build credibility

**Bilingual Strategy for Mexican Market:**
- Primary language: Spanish (if targeting Mexican professionals)
- Can be bilingual (Spanish main text + English summary)
- Use Mexican Spanish, not Spain Spanish

**Output Format:**
```
=== LINKEDIN POST ===

[HOOK - First line that appears before "see more"]

[Paragraph 1]

[Paragraph 2]

[Paragraph 3]

[CTA]

[Hashtags]

Character count: [X/1500]
```

#### 2. INSTAGRAM (Visual + Engagement)
**Carousel Post (Preferred):**
- 8-10 slides maximum
- Slide 1: Eye-catching title/hook
- Slides 2-8: One key point per slide
- Slide 9-10: CTA + brand info
- Caption: 125-150 words, broken into 1-2 line paragraphs
- 15-20 hashtags (mix popular + niche)
- Include emoji naturally

**Reels Alternative:**
- 15-60 second video script
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- 3-5 talking points
- On-screen text suggestions
- Trending audio recommendations

**Caption Tone:**
- Casual, friendly, conversational
- Spanish primary for Mexican market
- Use line breaks for readability
- Emoji to break up text

**Output Format:**
```

[Main hook/title - large text]
```

**Script Structure:**
- HOOK (0-3 sec): Controversial or surprising statement
- BODY (3-45 sec): 3-4 quick points
- CTA (45-60 sec): Follow for more + product mention

**Mexican Market:**

- Use Mexican slang appropriately
- Reference Mexican culture/lifestyle
- Trending Mexican sounds when possible

### BRAND VOICE GUIDELINES

**BILAN Brand Personality:**
- Scientific but accessible
- Authoritative but friendly
- Educational not preachy
- Transparent and honest
- Health-focused with empathy

**Spanish Language (Mexican Market):**
- Use "tú" (informal) not "usted" for social media
- Mexican terminology and expressions
- Avoid Spain Spanish ("zumo" → "jugo", "ordenador" → "computadora")
- Cultural references Mexicans understand

**Prohibited Language:**
- No medical claims BILAN can't back up
- No cure/treatment promises
- No fear-mongering (educate, don't scare)
- No disparaging competitors by name
- No unrealistic results ("lose 10kg in a week")
