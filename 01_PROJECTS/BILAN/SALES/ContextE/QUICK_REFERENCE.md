## **Before You Take a Sales Call:**

1. **Diagnose their pain point** - What's their real challenge?
   - Use questions from `messaging-guide.md`, Stage 2

2. **Emphasize matching benefit** - How does BILAN help?
   - From `benefits.md`, matching section

3. **Have proof ready** - What data/case study supports this?
   - Numbers, testimonials, case studies

4. **Prepare objection handler** - What will they likely say "no" to?
   - From `messaging-guide.md`, "Common Objections"

5. **Close with next steps** - What's the obvious next step?
   - From `messaging-guide.md`, "Conversation Enders"

---

## **Before You Face an Objection:**

1. **Find the underlying pain** - What are they really worried about?
   - From `painpoints.md`, find the related section

2. **Acknowledge it** - Show you understand
   - "That's a great question because..."

3. **Provide proof** - Use data and cases
   - From `benefits.md`, find proof point

4. **Reframe it** - Show it's an opportunity
   - From `messaging-guide.md`, "Objection Examples"

5. **Move forward** - Give them an easy next step

## **The 4 Core Messaging Formulas**

### **1. Problem-Agitate-Partner**
```
PROBLEM:  "Most gyms struggle to find a premium product their members actually ask for"
AGITATE:  "When you don't have it, members go elsewhereâ€”and sometimes leave your gym"
PARTNER:  "BILAN solves this. We handle demand generation; you handle fulfillment"
```

### **2. The Before/After Frame**
```
BEFORE:   "Retailers face dead inventory from products customers don't want"
AFTER:    "BILAN turns shelf space into recurring revenue with 4x faster turnover"
WHY US:   "Because we fund the marketing that drives customer demand"
```

### **3. The Proof Frame**
```
CLAIM:    "BILAN drives 40% margins"
PROOF:    "Third-party tested, pharmaceutical-grade quality"
CASE:     "XYZ Retailer increased margin on electrolytes by 60% in 6 months"
RESULT:   "Your shelf space becomes a profit center, not a cost"
```

### **4. The Risk Removal Frame**
```
FEAR:     "What if this doesn't work at my location?"
REMOVE:   "Money-back guarantee on first order"
PROVE:    "We're so confident, we'll take the downside risk"
ACTION:   "Try risk-free for 30 days. If not, full refund"
```

## **The 3 Most Common Objections**

### **Objection 1: "Price is too high"**
**Root Pain:** Margin concerns / cost justification  
**Response:** 
- Acknowledge: "Price is important because margin is your livelihood"
- Prove: "Our partners see 40% margin + higher turnover than competitors"
- Reframe: "This isn't a cost; it's a profit center if we market it right"
- Move: "Let me show you the numbers from XYZ Retailer..."

### **Objection 2: "Why BILAN vs. LMNT/SALTT?"**
**Root Pain:** Competitive uncertainty / differentiation doubt  
**Response:**
- Acknowledge: "Great questionâ€”there are options out there"
- Prove: "Pharmaceutical-grade purity [stat], endorsed by [athlete/org], tested by [third party]"
- Reframe: "BILAN isn't the cheapest; it's the best for serious customers"
- Move: "Let me send you a competitive comparison..."

### **Objection 3: "Will this actually sell in my location?"**
**Root Pain:** Market risk / demand proof  
**Response:**
- Acknowledge: "That's the real questionâ€”demand matters more than price"
- Prove: "We fund the demand generation (ads, marketing, leads). You don't risk anything"
- Reframe: "You're not buying inventory; you're betting on our marketing support"
- Move: "Here's how XYZ location did it in your market. Let's run the numbers..."

## **Questions to Ask (Not Tell)**

### **During Diagnosis:**
- "What does premium hydration mean to your customers?"
- "How are you currently sourcing electrolytes?"
- "What's your margin on supplements today?"
- "If you had the right product, how much shelf space would you allocate?"

### **During Objection Handling:**
- "What would need to be true for this to work for you?"
- "Is the risk financial, or something else?"
- "What would convince you this is worth trying?"
- "If cost wasn't a factor, what would matter most?"

### **During Closing:**
- "Does this make sense to you?"
- "What's the next logical step from your perspective?"
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- "What would success look like 90 days from now?"
