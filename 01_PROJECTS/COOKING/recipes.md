## ğŸ– Pan-Seared Pork Chops

### Ingredients
- 4 bone-in pork chops (1-inch thick)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1/4 cup chicken broth
- 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped

### Instructions
1. **Prep the Pork**: Pat pork chops dry with paper towels. Season generously with salt, pepper, thyme, and rosemary on both sides.

2. **Heat the Pan**: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering.

3. **Sear the Chops**: Carefully place pork chops in the hot pan. Sear for 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown and crusty.

4. **Add Aromatics**: Reduce heat to medium. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

5. **Finish Cooking**: Add butter and chicken broth to the pan. Simmer for 5-7 minutes, basting the chops occasionally.

6. **Rest**: Transfer pork chops to a plate and let rest for 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley.

### Tips
- Use a meat thermometer for perfect results (145Â°F internal temperature)
- Don't overcrowd the pan - cook in batches if necessary
- The pan sauce is perfect for drizzling over the pork

## ğŸ— Perfect Roasted Chicken

### Ingredients
- 1 whole chicken (3-4 pounds)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
- 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 lemon, halved
- 1 teaspoon salt
- Â½ teaspoon black pepper
- 1 cup chicken broth or white wine

### Instructions
1. **Prep Chicken**: Remove giblets. Pat chicken dry inside and out with paper towels.
2. **Season**: Mix olive oil, rosemary, thyme, garlic, salt, and pepper. Rub all over chicken, including cavity.
3. **Preheat**: Preheat oven to 425Â°F.
4. **Arrange**: Place chicken in roasting pan, breast side up. Put lemon halves in cavity.
5. **Roast**: Roast for 1 hour and 15-20 minutes, or until thermometer reads 165Â°F in thickest part of thigh.
6. **Rest**: Let chicken rest for 10-15 minutes before carving.

### Pan Gravy
1. Pour off excess fat from roasting pan.
2. Place pan on stovetop over medium heat.
3. Add broth or wine and scrape up browned bits.
4. Simmer for 5 minutes, strain, and serve with chicken.

### Tips
- A room temperature chicken cooks more evenly
- Truss the legs for even cooking
- Use a meat thermometer for foolproof results
- Save pan drippings for gravy
